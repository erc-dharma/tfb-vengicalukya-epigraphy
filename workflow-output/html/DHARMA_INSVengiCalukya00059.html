Apparatus

Seal

Plates

01 svasti NR • According to NR’s note, this word is written at the beginning of the third line.
1 -nivāsa NR • All known grants of Jayasiṁha I mentioning Asanapura use the term asanapura-vāsakāt.
2 vikāṣatānāṁ tṛvu vāna NR • The correct reading may be mahāsenenābhivarddhitānān tribhuvana-mātr̥bhir mmātr̥bhir abhirakṣitānāṁ. Compare line 2 of the Timmapuram plates of Viṣṇuvardhana I.
4 °(sya) • Normally, Kīrtīvarman’s name is in the standard genitive kīrttīvarmaṇaḥ. The non-standard form kīrttīvarmasya may have been used here, or the correct reading might be ya, for kīrttīvarmaṇaḥ priya-naptā, as in the Guḍivāḍa plates (set 2) of Jayasiṁha I. — 4 vi° • I wonder if this character is in fact an initial Ai, as in the Guḍivāḍa plates (set 2) of Jayasiṁha I where kīrttīvarmaṇaḥ priya-naptā is followed by Aidaṁyugīna-mahā-viṣṇoḥ viṣṇuvarddhana-mahārājasya priya-tanayaḥ.
6 samā NR • In the Guḍivāḍa plates (set 2) of Jayasiṁha I, which seem to be a close parallel for this grant, the term pravarddhamāna-pratāpopanata-samasta-sāmanta-maṇḍalaḥ describing Jayasiṁha is followed by sva-śakti-traya-triśūlāvabhinna-para-narapati-sakala-bala-cetanaḥ. NR’s samā may thus be a misreading of sva-śa.
7 sarvva-siddhaḥ NR • NR is probably correct in assuming that this is the title sarvasiddhi, but this title of Jayasiṁha I is never, as far as I presently know, explained with apratihata-śaktitva.
8 nātavāḍyāM ◇ nātavādhyāM NR • The spelling of this name is nātavāḍi in the ARIE report and natavāḍi in NR’s discussion. Other Eastern Cālukya grants use nātavāḍi or nātāvāḍi. — 8 Uccāṭi- ◇ Uccāti- NR • I adopt the name as reported in ARIE, which is usually reliable. NR only uses the dental t both in his Devanagari edition and in his Romanisation in the discussion.
9 dvi-dvādaśa- • NR repeats this phrase in his discussion, interpreting it to mean twice 12 khaṇḍikās. This is possible, but not paralleled in any related grant that I know. I wonder if dvi is in fact deleted.
11 putrasya • I wonder if the word intended here was in fact pautrāya. This would result in a more typical formulation of the donee’s descent, and would also eliminate the strangeness of father and son bearing the same name.
13 -nimitt¡e!⟨a I⟩yam • Scribal omission is also possible here; the composer’s intent may have been nimitteneyam. However, since related grants normally use nimitte in the locative, it is more likely that this is a case of non-standard sandhi, or possibly of inaccurate gender use (nimitte + ayam). — 13 kenaci¡T! bādhā • NR’s actual spelling in his edition is kenacit bādhā (with a tb conjunct), but I assume that the original has a final T here.
19 traloka mahādevuka sammukhaṁ • This reading is doubtful. According to NR’s discussion, the grant was made ‘in the presence of the Queen Triloka Mahādevī’. I know of no such queen, and even NR seems not to trust his own reading/interpretation, since according to his introduction, the grant does not contain ‘any new unknown or additional information about the Eastern Cālukyan chronology or genealogy’. If the reading can be checked against the original and a queen by this name is indeed indicated, then this is important new information. More likely, the correct reading is something else. One of Jayasiṁha I’s epithets is trailokya-vikrama, which may have been featured or indicated here. As for sammukhaṁ, I wonder if the correct reading is in fact samukha (for sva-mukha). — 19–20 bu/[1×](ḍi) buggi āṇati • According to NR’s discussion, the executor was called Buggi. He does not discuss the damaged characters before these.

Plates

01Greetings!

1-7[From] the majestic residence at Asanapura. The grandson [of Kīrtivarman, who ... the family of] the Calukyas—who are ¿empowered? [by Mahāsena], [who are protected by the Mothers who are mothers of] ¿the three worlds? [... who are of the Mānavya go]tra [...]—[the son of] His Majesty Viṣṇuvardhana, who [...] circle↓1 [...] King [Jaya]siṁha Vallabha, who [has subdued] the circle of rival rulers (sāmanta) [...] who is all-accomplishing (sarva-siddhi) thanks to his incontestable power [...]

7-14The [plot of] land named ¿Sarvvarddhi Relluvaṭṭi? on the eastern boundary of the village named Uccāṭi in Nātavāḍi [district], to the extent [sufficient for sowing] twice twelve↓2 khaṇḍikas of seed—this [plot of] land has been given [by us, Jayasiṁha] with exemption from all taxes, in order to augment the merit of our mother and father and ourselves on the occasion of vyatīpāta↓3 in the month of Vaiśākha, to Mātr̥śarman, a knower of the Veda learned in six śāstras, son of Mātr̥śarman who was a knower of pada recitation [and who was in turn] son of Durganandiśarman,↓4 a knower of two Vedas residing in Vaṁgipaṟu, of the Āpastamba school and the Kaṇva gotra. Let no-one pose an obstacle (to his enjoyment of his rights over it). There are moreover [these] ślokas sung by Vyāsa.

I.
Many (kings) have granted land, and many have preserved it (as formerly granted). Whosoever at any time owns the land, the fruit {reward (accrued of granting it)} belongs to him at that time.

II.
A donor of land stays in heaven for sixty millennia, [while] a seizer [of granted land] and a condoner [of such seizure] shall reside in hell for just as many.

III.
He who would seize land, whether given by himself or by another, imbibes the sin of the slayer of a hundred thousand cows.

19-20¿In the presence of Traloka Mahādevuka.? ↓5 The executor (āṇati) is Bu[1×]ḍibuggi.

Bibliography

Reported in ARIE 1997-1998: page 18, appendix A/1997–98, № 5 without discussion of details. Edited from the original plates by N. Ramesan (1988: № A), without facsimiles and without translation. The present edition by Dániel Balogh thus follows the published edition, silently correcting only obvious typos↓6 and offering suggestions for improvement in the apparatus.

Notes

↑1. This was probably a reference to subduing the circle of rival rulers, to conquering the circle of the earth, or to perfuming the circle of the horizon with his glory.
↑2. Or perhaps just twelve; see the apparatus to line 9.
↑3. Vyatīpāta is an astronomical moment at which the declinations of the moon and sun are of equal magnitude, related to the prediction of eclipses (Shylaja and Kydala 2016).
↑4. It is possible that the text is erroneous here (see the apparatus to line 11); the donee’s father was perhaps Durganandiśarman, and the donee perhaps bore the same name as his grandfather Mātr̥śarman.
↑5. See the apparatus to line 19.
↑6. The text of the edition is printed in Devanagari, but was apparently typeset from a Romanised manuscript, giving rise to mistakes such as सिम्ह instead of सिंह.
