Hands:

Halantas. l1, T: if correctly read, this is a very small character at baseline, without a line above. L8 matimāN: also very small at baseline, and seems to have a _vertical_ line above it. L12 gotrābhyāM: indistinct. L19 vaseT: small at baseline; may have a fairly long stem upward.

Original punctuation. l16, a short vertical floating at mid-height. L17 same. The closing symbol appears to be composed of three dots in a triangular arrangement.

Apparatus

1 svasti{ḥ} • Fleet notes that what looks like a visarga is probably a punctuation mark, “imperfectly cleared out." He probably refers to the physical cleaning of the plates. In the scanned estampage, the presence of a visarga seems quite certain. — 1 śrīma¡(T)!-calukya- ◇ śrīmac-⟨c⟩alukya- JFFFleet 1891; śrīm(ān) calukya- ACBBurnell 1878 • Though not clear in the scan, it seems likely that there is a halanta T here. Burnell may have seen it is a halanta N, but his text implies a conjunct with n. No ā is discernible in the facsimile.

Bibliography

First brought to notice by Master in 1867, who sent the plates to the Madras Government. Described and translated (but not edited) by T. Foulkes (). First edited (probably from estampages) by A. C. Burnell (137-13827) with a lithograph (of an inked estampage?) in Plate 27. Re-edited by J. F. Fleet () by which time the plates had been lost, so Fleet worked from Burnell’s lithograph. PEM’s record says Fleet’s edition includes a facsimile, but this does not seem to be the case; CHECK a printed copy of IA20. The present edition by Dániel Balogh is based on a collation of the above editions with the estampages published by Burnell.
