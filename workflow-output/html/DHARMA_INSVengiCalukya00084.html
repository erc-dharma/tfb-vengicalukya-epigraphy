Original punctuation marks.

Other palaeographic observations. Anusvāra is at or slightly above headline, to the right of the character to which it belongs. The plates often do not distinguish dependent au from o (but see e.g. line 9 kaḷadhauta for a distinct au); RM silently reads the expected vowel, while I show a scribal error. Upadhmānīya in line 11 looks identical to ṟ.

6 (sva)[?8+] ◇ (a)[?8+] RM • The lost portion is far too short to accommodate a version of the compound beginning with svāsi-dhārā- that typically occurs at this point. The title sarvva-lokāśraya may have been used here, but on its own it is too short for the gap and the partially preserved character at the edge of the plate seems to be sva. There may have been a garbled, very short version of the svāsi-dhārā- compound here.
