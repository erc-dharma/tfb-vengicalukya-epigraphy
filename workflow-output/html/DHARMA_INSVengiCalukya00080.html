Translation by Dániel Balogh

Seal

Plates

1-10Greetings. The grandson of His Majesty King Viṣṇuvardhana (II), who was eager to adorn the lineage of the majestic Chaḷukyas—who are of the Mānavya gotra which is praised by the entire world, who are sons of Hārīti, who attained kingship by the grace of Kauśikī’s boon, who were deliberately appointed (to kingship) by Lord Mahāsena, who are protected by the band of Mothers, who acquired the superior Boar emblem by the grace of the divine Nārāyaṇa, and who perform the Aśvamedha sacrifice—the dear son of His Majesty Maṅgi Yuvarāja: His Majesty the supremely pious King Viṣṇuvardhana (III), whose pair of feet are tinted by the hues of the rays from the gems fitted to the crowns of enemy kings bowed down by the blade of his sword, who was deliberately appointed (as heir) by his mother and father, commands everyone [as follows].

10-25Let it be known to you that, ¿being pleased in [our] heart with the instruction concerning the desired fruit of endeavour (artha-phala), [given to us] by? the Reverend Master (ācārya) Kālibhadra, who has, through his eightfold divine knowledge subjugated the entire circle of kings, ¿and who is the disciple of? the Master Devanandi, ¿who was the disciple of? the Master Kamalabhadra, ¿who was the disciple of? the Master Rāvinandi, ¿who was the disciple of? the Master Ravicandra, ¿who was the disciple of? the Reverend Master Candraprabha in the monastic lineage of the venerable gaṇa of Kavuṟūr in the Surasta gaṇa—[according to which instruction] we shall attain even the position of supreme sovereign through succession on the morning of the dark [fortnight’s] twelfth [day] of the month Mārgaśīrṣa”—in order to augment our longevity, health, virtue (dharma) and reputation, we [Viṣṇuvardhana III] have given the village called by the name Musinikuṇḍa in the prosperous Toṁka-Nātavāḍi district (viṣaya), exempt from all taxes, to the Naḍuṁbi monastery (vasati) of Bijavaḍa (Vijayavāṭa)—[which was commissioned by] the Great Queen Ayyana, the dear beloved of His Majesty the King Kubja Viṣṇuvardhana (I)—¿for the purpose of facilitating the? uninterrupted worship (pūjā) of the Reverend Arhats, [the donation being] sanctified by a libation of water ¿[performed] with (i.e. into the hands of)? the Reverend Master Kālibhadra.↓1

25-31The borders of this village [are as follows]. To the east, it is up to Aruvalapallama. To the south, it is up to ¿[the line extending] from the tamarind tree behind the Kusuma-guṇḍa to the border of Ṟāyi and Rāvi-guṇḍa. To the west, it is up to the border of Mroṁguṟayi. To the north, it is up to the border of rāceṟuvula cilloḻka-pallavu. Thus it is demarcated by four boundaries.?

31-39The borders of this village, the borders in the intercardinal directions [are as follows].↓2 To the southeast, ¿it is up to [the line extending] fom Viriṁguṇḍa and the Mrovula tank to the uñca pannasa of tūbhāmogaḍlanūyi?. In the southwestern direction, ¿the boundary is up to [the line extending] from the western cāṭṟayi of Rāvi-guṇḍa to the border of Elamañci and Kuṭṟu-kallu?. In the northwestern direction, ¿the boundary is up to the border of Ṭiggiṟayi?. In the northeastern direction, ¿the boundary is up to the border of Muvvuṁ-ḍoṁka and Podalu?. Thus it is demarcated by four boundaries in the intercardinal directions.

39-42⟨Let⟩ no-one ⟨pose⟩ an obstacle (to the enjoyment of rights) over it. ⟨He who⟩ does so shall be conjoined with the five great sins. ¿There is no conflict of interest concerning the sāda and ari-sāda (taxes) applicable to this village.?↓3 Vyāsa has said:

I.
He who would seize land, whether given by himself or by another, shall be born as a worm in faeces for sixty thousand years.

II.
Many (kings) have granted land, and many have preserved it (as formerly granted). Whosoever at any time owns the land, the fruit {reward} (accrued of granting it) belongs to him at that time.

46-48The extent of all the fields at this village amounts to that sowable with a thousand and thirty-five [measures of] kodrava seed.

III.
May there be blessing for the sin-destroying teaching of the Kings of Victors (jinendra): the sun that splits asunder the clouds arising from the darkness [exuded by] unworthy [teachers].↓4

50-52The executor (ājñapti) is the Great Queen Ayyaṇa.↓5 ¿[This] decree is marked [with the seal of] King Kubja Viṣṇuvardhana.?

53-55The extent of the circumference of the homestead [plot is as follows]. To the east, the road. To the south, nāgula-paṇḍu. To the west, ¿a rock-cut shelter?. To the north, koṇḍa lāya. pesa ledu.

Notes

↑1. My interpretation of this passage is tentative and radically different from that of the ARIE report and its previous editor, as well as all scholars that I am aware of who cited this grant in secondary literature before its publication. The passage is a single, exceedingly complex sentence with numerous scribal mistakes and thoroughly garbled case endings. See the apparatus to lines 11 to 23 for the textual problems, and the commentary for a discussion of the interpretation.
↑2. Most of my translations in this passage are desperate attempts to make sense of an incoherent mixture of Telugu and bad Sanskrit, and may well be off in many places.
↑3. This sentence may mean something entirely different. A tax named sāda appears in Sircar’s Indian Epigraphical Glossary, attested in an inscription of the Kalyāṇī Cālukya yuvarāja Mallikārjuna.
↑4. There may be a double entendre to the latter part of the stanza, which might also be understood as the (real) sun splitting apart clouds of mist rising from a river at an unpleasant ford (kutīrtha). However, dhvānta does not mean “mist,” so such an interpretation is forced.
↑5. See the commentary.

Bibliography

Reported in ARIE 1916-1917: page 7, appendix A/1916–17, № 9 with discussion at ARIE 1916-1917: page 114, № 21. Edited from estampages by Vepa Ramesam (1945-1946: № C), with facsimiles, with a rather desultory summary of the contents. The present edition by Dániel Balogh is based on a collation of Ramesam’s edition with his estampages. The published edition is replete with typographic errors and inconsistent and idiosyncratic transliteration and spelling.
