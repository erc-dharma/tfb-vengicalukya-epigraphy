Identifier:

INSVengiCalukya00083

Hands:

Halantas. Final M (l27) is a simplified ma. Final T (l27) is a reduced ta with a short vertical tail instead of a headmark.

Original punctuation marks.

Other palaeographic observations. Anusvāra is normally above and to the right of the character to which it belongs. Long ī is hardly ever distinct (but occasionally it is, e.g. l8 kīrtteḥ); RM’s edition silently reads the ī where appropriate, while my edition shows the short i used instead of long ī as a scribal mistake. Rare initial Au occurs in line 22, though it may have been written as O. RM reads it as Au and there is a faint curved stroke at the top right that, if genuine, distinguishes it from O.

His point is not to corroborate this reading but to indicate that the title is simpler there. It is certain on the basis of several cognate plates that the intended expression was prabhāvāvarjjitāśeṣa-.

However, I find -kiraṇa-kara- implausible, while there may easily have been a now indistinct sa at the beginning of line 7.

24 -vākyā¿t?⟨rth⟩a-pramana- ◇ -vākya{ta}-pram¿a?⟨ā⟩¿n?⟨ṇ⟩a- RM • I find RM’s emendation unsatisfactory. I am confident that vākyāta is a scribal mistake for vākyārtha or vākyārttha, and the vowel of kyā is quite clear. I am less sure about pramana, but believe that it may be erroneous for pravacana, with one character omitted and either va or ca mistakenly changed to ma. It could also be derived similarly, if less easily, from pracakṣaṇa. Other solutions may well be possible, so I prefer not to show any emendation in the text. Furthermore, the superfluous śa above the characters prabha after this word may be relevant. I show read this as śśa in the previous line, while R apparently ignores it. This could perhaps be a correction intended for this locus, though see also the apparatus to line 25.
25 Āyu¡-m!⟨r⟩-ārogy(ā) • I believe this m is not an unwitting mistake but non-standard sandhi involving an epenthetic m. — 25 (bhav)vaś{(ś)}a⟨r⟩ma¿(na)?⟨ṇe⟩ • I accept RM’s reading of the received text, which he prints as clear. In the published estampage it is very much unclear; I cannot see a subscript ś at all, though compare line 23 above. RM emends the name to Bhavaśarman, but I prefer not to do so.
26 Ī(ṇḍ)u(ba)ḷḷi ◇ reṇḍubaḷḷi RM • The name was read as Raṇḍubaḷḷi for the ARIE report, while RM asserts that it is correctly Reṇḍubaḷḷi. See also the commentary. In my opinion the two dots on either side of the ra-like glyph leave no doubt that it is a long initial Ī. As far as I can see from the printed estampage, the second character could be ndu as well as ṇḍu, while the third is quite indistinct.
27–28 tā(ṟka) /ṭ¿u?⟨ū⟩ru • I adopt RM’s emendation in the name and accept his reading for the last character of line 26, where all I see is a faint descending hook that may be part of a subscript k or a dependent u. RM also prints his ṟka as unclear.
28–29 (jakka) /nace◯ṟuvu • The last two characters of line 28 cannot be made out at all in the printed estampage. RM also prints them as unclear.

I believe the text is garbled from expressions involving Eteṣāṁ catur-avadhi-madhya-varti (cf. the Nemmaḷūru and Dinakādu grants of Vijayāditya I).
